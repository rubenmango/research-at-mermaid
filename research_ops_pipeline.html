01 Executive Summary

Two teams are running parallel research operations with fourteen tools, significant manual overhead, and a broken data foundation. The stack is capable but fragmented. Most research is episodic rather than continuous. Gut decisions are still common despite tooling.

The single biggest blocker is Mixpanel data inaccuracy, which undermines every quantitative decision downstream. Thirteen operational gaps have been identified — three are critical blockers today. Eleven product issues are known but completely unvalidated.

With targeted automation across seven workflows, the team can shift from monthly research cycles to a near-continuous insight pipeline with minimal added headcount — reclaiming roughly 179 hours per month and around $178,560 in annual value.

The stack is comprehensive but the tools don't talk to each other. Every step from Mixpanel to a Jira ticket requires a human.

07 Where the teams converge

Six points
of shared truth.

Today, every team writes its own questions, runs its own sessions, and stores its own findings. After the overhaul, six shared layers connect everything.

i.

Vistaly · source of truth

Single repository for all research signals. All teams route findings here, no exceptions.

ii.

Shared question bank

Notion database tagged by theme and method. Slack bot surfaces relevant questions at sprint planning.

iii.

AI synthesis pipeline

Same Claude prompt, same taxonomy, same output format — regardless of team or method.

iv.

#research Slack channel

One channel broadcasting findings across teams. 5-bullet summary auto-posted after every session.

v.

Auto Jira tickets

Both teams' sessions auto-create cards with source citations and confidence levels. No manual writeup.

vi.

Experiment cross-reference

A/B result + interview quotes + survey response = one unified Vistaly card per experiment.

08 Growth experiments as one pool

What happened.
Why it happened.
How it felt.

Three signals that today live in three places and are never combined. After: every experiment generates one Vistaly card containing the metric movement, the qualitative explanation, and the user's reaction.

What happened

Statsig A/B results

Metric movement and confidence intervals — tells you if something worked.

Why it happened

User interviews

Qualitative context from the same cohort — tells you why it worked or failed.

How it felt

Typeform in-app

Behavioral context and immediate reaction — tells you how users experienced it.

10 Automation Roadmap · 7 actions

Seven steps,
in this order.

Sequenced by prerequisite dependency. Step 1 is non-negotiable — every quantitative decision downstream depends on reliable data.

01

Fix the data foundation

Run a full audit of every Mixpanel and Statsig event. Build a canonical event taxonomy in Notion with definitions, fire conditions, and owners. Set up a weekly Zapier cron that monitors fire rates and posts anomalies to a #data-health Slack channel. Add accuracy stamps to all dashboards.

ToolsMixpanel · Statsig · Zapier · Slack · Notion
Effort3–5 days
02

One master dashboard

Archive every dashboard except one canonical Mixpanel board tracking WAU/MAU, conversion funnel, feature adoption (predefined prompts, templates, AI chat), plugin-to-platform rate, and D1/D7/D30 retention. Weekly Monday Slack post with a 3-number summary.

ToolsMixpanel · Slack · Zapier
Effort2 days
03

Automate the interview pipeline

Zapier chain: Mixpanel cohort → Apollo enrichment → email sequence → Calendly booking → Zoom auto-created → post-interview gift card trigger. Converts ~4 hrs/week of manual work to ~15 min review. Target 2–4 interviews/week running passively.

ToolsZapier · Mixpanel · Apollo · Calendly · Zoom
Effort2–3 days
04

AI research synthesis agent

After each interview: Claude API runs on the transcript to tag themes, extract verbatim quotes, flag product issues with severity, auto-create Vistaly card and Jira ticket with source citation and confidence level, and post a 5-bullet summary to #research. The team is already partially doing this — fully operationalize it.

ToolsClaude API · Zapier · Vistaly · Jira · Slack · Notion
Effort3–4 days
05

Continuous in-app feedback loops

Typeform micro-surveys triggered by behavioral events: first diagram created, 3-day return visit, export action, abandoned upgrade click, 7-day anniversary. Two questions max each. Route responses via Zapier into the Notion research repo, tagged with cohort and timestamp.

ToolsTypeform · Zapier · Notion
Effort~1 week (5 triggers)
06

Async testing on every feature ship

Maze test templates for the 5 critical flows: onboarding, first diagram, AI chat, export and share, and plan upgrade. After each ship that touches one of these flows, auto-trigger the relevant Maze test via API. 20–40 testers per run. Results feed Vistaly via webhook.

ToolsMaze · Zapier · Vistaly · CI/CD
Effort2 days/template + 1 day wiring
07

Shared question bank + sprint surfacing

Notion database of research questions tagged by theme (onboarding, conversion, AI features, retention, editor UX) and method (interview, survey, A/B, usability). Slack bot scans sprint Jira tickets and surfaces the 5 most relevant questions at sprint planning. Stops starting every interview from scratch.

ToolsNotion · Jira · Slack · Zapier
Effort1–2 days
