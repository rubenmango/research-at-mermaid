How to run a design test
in 10 minutes.

The minimum-viable path from "I have a prototype" to "I have user feedback in 24 hours." Five common flows are pre-baked. New flows take ~10 minutes once.

TL;DR

Host your prototype somewhere with a public URL → open the matching Great Question template → paste the URL into the website-test block → publish. Results land in 24 hours.

New in v0.2.0 · sprint-discovery Before you test, ask Claude what we already know. Trigger phrases like "sprint discovery for predefined prompts" or "what do we already know about onboarding?". Claude generates an R-OPS-G-{topic} Google Doc with a TL;DR, prior insights, strongest quotes, and the top unanswered questions — then asks you whether to post a heads-up to #on-going-design-research and/or open a Jira ticket on your team's board. You decide what gets broadcast. The doc is the source of truth either way.

01 Before you start

You need three things ready:

  • A prototype with a public URL. Figma share link, Vercel preview, Netlify drop, or GitHub Pages — any of these work. Claude artifact URLs are session-private; deploy them to Netlify or GH Pages first.
  • Access to the Mermaid Great Question workspace. If you don't have it, ping #design-ops.
  • The research question. One sentence: what do you want to learn? If you can't write it in one sentence, you're not ready to test yet.

02 Pick the matching template

Five templates live in the GQ workspace as drafts. Each has the research goal, task script, screener, and incentive already written. Open the one that matches what you're testing:

03 Duplicate, paste, publish

~30 sec
Step 3a

Duplicate the template

In Great Question, open the template → click Duplicate → rename it with the date and feature (e.g. Onboarding · 2026-05 · new welcome screen).

~30 sec
Step 3b

Paste your prototype URL into the website-test block

Build tab → find the website-test block → replace the placeholder URL with your prototype link. Don't change anything else. The task script, screener, and incentive are already set.

~1 min
Step 3c

Sanity-check and publish

Review tab → confirm screener reads cleanly and the URL is right. Recruit tab → pick the participant source (panel by default) → publish.

That's the whole authoring loop. The first response usually lands within 24 hours.

04 When results land

You'll get a Slack ping in #research when the first responses come in. From there:

  • Review the recordings in GQ. The transcript is auto-generated.
  • Highlight the moments that matter — GQ's highlight feature clips them for the repo.
  • The synthesis runs automatically — Claude tags themes, extracts quotes, creates a Vistaly card and a Jira ticket, posts a 5-bullet summary to #research. You don't write it up.
  • Your job: read, decide, ship.

05 New flow (no matching template)

If none of the five templates fit, ~10 minutes:

  • Ask Claude (in Cowork) to draft three task statements for your feature. Give it the Jira ticket as context.
  • Create a new unmoderated study in GQ. Title, research goal, slot cap of 12, $25–35 incentive.
  • Add a website-test block with your prototype URL and the three Claude-drafted tasks.
  • Attach the standard consent form and screener ("Have you used diagramming tools recently?" qualifies most cases).
  • Publish. Same result loop as step 4.
One-time templates worth saving. If the new flow is one you'll run again, save the study as a template in GQ — it joins the five pre-baked ones.

06 Common questions

Can I test on the live product instead of a prototype?

Yes. The Phase 03 post-ship variant exists for exactly this — point the same template at app.mermaidchart.com instead of a prototype URL. Catches drift between design and engineering.

What incentive should I use?

The five templates have incentives baked in ($25–$35 depending on the test). For new flows, $25 is the floor and $40 is the ceiling unless the test is over 20 minutes or asks something unusual.

How many participants?

Cap at 12 by default. Past 8–10 responses you hit diminishing returns for unmoderated tests. Stop early if you're seeing the same pattern.

Something looks wrong with my prototype URL.

Test the URL in an incognito window first — if you can't access it logged out, neither can participants. For Figma, use the share link with view permissions set to "anyone with the link." For Vercel/Netlify, the preview URLs are public by default.

Help, I broke something.

Ping #design-ops. Or revert by deleting the duplicate and starting fresh — the original templates can't be damaged from a duplicate.
